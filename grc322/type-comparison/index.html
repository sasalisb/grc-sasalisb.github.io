Officina vs. FF Meta

History of each typeface

Officina

Spiekermann created Officina in 1990 in Southern Italy. He hopes to create a family of type that would be well-suited for business documentation and correspondence. When ITC Officina was produced, this typeface proved to have capabilities beyond its original intention.

FF Meta

FF Meta was designed by Erik Spiekermann in 1991 at the West German Post Office. The typeface was intended to be a “antithesis of Helvetica.”

Comparison

Similarities

FF Meta and ITC Officina Sans share some similarities, such as their legibility and modern design approach, however they have subtle but distinctive difference in their visual style and personality.

Differences

Meta is a more modern and versatile typeface that is suitable for a wider range of applications. While Officina, on the other hand, has a more classic feel, making it well-suited to editorial and publishing work. Looking at both typefaces from afar, it really doesn’t look like there is much of a difference between the two, however, when you take a closer look at the “g” for example, there is a significant difference between the two typefaces. Another note to add is that the ends of the upper-case strokes (Y, X, W, K) are slanted for Officina and horizontal for Meta.
